## üìñ Overview
The **GTC Gatekeeper** framework shifts AI safety from voluntary "Soft Law" to the enforceable rigor of **Global Trade Compliance (GTC)**. By treating high-performance compute (HPC) as a physically monitorable precursor to frontier AI, this framework establishes a verifiable **Standard of Care** for the digital age.



---

## ‚ú® The Three Pillars of the Framework

### 1. The Physical supply chain Anchor
Frontier-scale training (>$10^{26}$ FLOPs) requires massive industrial infrastructure. These data centers leave "heat signatures" and energy footprints detectable via satellite imagery and grid-usage patterns. This allows for oversight at the technological root, even when the software output remains diffuse.

### 2. The Anti-Bystander Mandate
This mandate redefines Cloud Service Providers (CSPs) as **Statutory Sentinels**. They are no longer "neutral pipes" but hold a legal **Duty of Inquiry** to perform "Workload KYC." This requires investigating compute patterns consistent with unauthorized "Red Line" activities, such as autonomous biological weapon simulations.



### 3. Telemetry-Linked Compliance (TLC)
TLC replaces passive reporting with **Compliance by Design**. It utilizes firmware-level locks and industrial metadata to verify geographic location and workload integrity without compromising private code or user data.

## üõ†Ô∏è Red Flag Checklist for GTC Practitioners
To identify suspicious compute transactions, practitioners should monitor for the following behavioral indicators:

* üö© **Cluster Stacking**: Aggregating mid-tier chips (below export thresholds) in massive quantities to build unvetted frontier-scale supercomputers.
* üö© **Interconnect Discrepancy**: Requests for high-performance cabling (e.g., InfiniBand) that are disproportionate to the stated "basic business" use.
* üö© **Audit Reluctance**: Refusal to accept "No Re-export" clauses or "Post-Export Verification" (PEV) audit requirements.

## ‚öñÔ∏è Strategic Bounds (Refine & Bound)
This framework is positioned as a **Necessary but Partial Safeguard**:
* **Deceleration Tool**: While algorithmic efficiency may eventually bypass hardware gates, compute governance buys "diplomatic time" to build secondary software-level norms.
* **Non-Surveillance**: TLC focuses on **industrial footprints**, maintaining a clear boundary between trade compliance and systemic user surveillance.
* **Multilateral Stability**: Establishing a common technical baseline for global stability to prevent a "race to the bottom" in safety standards.
